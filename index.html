Policy Alert

Biden Signs Executive Order Requiring AI Model Registration

The order: President Biden signed Executive Order 14129 requiring any AI company training models with more than 10^25 FLOPs to register with the Department of Commerce within 90 days. The order establishes mandatory safety testing protocols, algorithmic auditing requirements, and incident reporting systems for frontier AI models, with penalties including $10 million fines and restricted access to federal computing resources.

The requirements: Companies must submit detailed training methodologies, safety evaluation results, and risk mitigation strategies before deploying models publicly. The order creates a new AI Safety Review Board within Commerce, empowered to halt model releases deemed to pose national security or public safety risks, while requiring quarterly capability assessments for models exceeding performance thresholds.

The reaction: Tech industry leaders expressed mixed reactions, with Anthropic and OpenAI supporting the framework while Meta and Google criticized the registration thresholds as overly restrictive. Republican lawmakers immediately announced plans to challenge the order in court, arguing it exceeds executive authority and could handicap US AI competitiveness against China's less regulated development approach.

Research, Translated

DeepMind Solves Protein Folding for All Known Life Forms

The breakthrough: DeepMind's AlphaFold 3 has successfully predicted the 3D structures of over 200 million proteins across all known life forms, completing what researchers call the 'protein universe map.' Published in Nature, the achievement covers every protein in major biological databases, from viruses to complex mammals, with accuracy levels above 90% for most predictions, dramatically expanding from AlphaFold 2's coverage of 1 million structures.

Why it matters: This comprehensive protein structure database could revolutionize drug discovery, allowing pharmaceutical companies to design medications by understanding exactly how drugs interact with target proteins. Researchers estimate this could reduce drug development timelines from 10-15 years to 3-5 years, while enabling entirely new approaches to treating diseases like Alzheimer's, cancer, and rare genetic disorders that were previously considered undruggable.

The applications: Beyond medicine, the protein map enables breakthroughs in biotechnology, including designing enzymes for plastic degradation, creating more efficient biofuels, and developing sustainable materials. DeepMind is releasing the entire database freely to researchers, with pharmaceutical giants like Novartis and Roche already announcing new drug programs based on previously unknown protein structures discovered through AlphaFold 3's predictions.
