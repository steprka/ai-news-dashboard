The product: Slack launched 'Invisible Assistant,' an AI tool that generates meeting summaries and action items without actually joining video calls. The system analyzes Slack messages, calendar invites, and document activity around meeting times to infer what happened, eliminating the awkward 'AI bot has joined' notifications that users have grown to hate.

The market move: This represents a clever end-run around meeting fatigue and privacy concerns that have plagued AI note-taking tools like Otter.ai and Notion AI. AI Discovers How to Make Lithium Batteries Last 10x Longer

The breakthrough: Researchers at UC Berkeley used machine learning to identify optimal lithium battery charging patterns that extend battery life by 900% compared to standard methods. The AI analyzed millions of charge cycles to discover that specific voltage fluctuations and temperature controls can dramatically slow chemical degradation inside battery cells.

The method: Instead of trying to understand the complex chemistry, the AI treated batteries as black boxes and experimented with thousands of different charging strategies on real cells. The system discovered counterintuitive patterns - like briefly overcharging then undercharging - that somehow prevent the formation of dendrites that typically kill battery performance over time.

The impact: This could revolutionize everything from smartphones to electric vehicles, potentially eliminating range anxiety and reducing electronic waste. Tesla and other EV manufacturers are already in talks to license the technology, while smartphone makers see an opportunity to market phones that maintain peak battery performance for decades instead of years.
