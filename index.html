Research, Translated

AI Learns Human Emotions by Watching Eye Movement Patterns

The discovery: Researchers at Stanford developed an AI system that can identify human emotions with 92% accuracy just by tracking eye movements. The system analyzes subtle patterns like pupil dilation, blink rates, and gaze direction to distinguish between happiness, sadness, anger, fear, and surprise.

How it works: The AI was trained on eye-tracking data from 10,000 people watching emotional video clips. It learned that fear creates rapid, scattered eye movements, while sadness produces slower, more focused gazing patterns. The system can detect emotions in real-time using standard webcams or phone cameras.

Why this matters: This breakthrough could revolutionize mental health diagnosis, educational technology, and human-computer interaction. Unlike facial recognition, eye-tracking works even when people try to hide their emotions, potentially enabling earlier detection of depression, anxiety, and other psychological conditions through routine screen time.

Nature, Science Daily
