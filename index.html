Policy Alert

France Becomes First EU Nation to License AI Models Like Pharmaceuticals

The new law: France's parliament passed groundbreaking legislation requiring AI models above a certain capability threshold to undergo safety testing and obtain government licenses before deployment. The law treats powerful AI systems similarly to prescription drugs, requiring clinical trials and ongoing monitoring.

The requirements: AI companies must submit safety data, conduct bias testing, and pay annual licensing fees starting at €50,000 for basic models up to €2 million for frontier AI systems. Models that fail safety tests face immediate suspension, while companies can lose licenses for non-compliance with transparency requirements.

The ripple effects: Tech giants including Google and OpenAI criticized the law as overly restrictive and threatened to limit services in France. However, German and Italian lawmakers announced similar bills in development, suggesting France's approach could become the EU standard ahead of broader AI Act implementation.

AI Learns to Feel Physical Sensations Through Robot Bodies

The breakthrough: Researchers at UC Berkeley developed an AI system that can experience touch, temperature, and pressure through robotic sensors, essentially giving artificial intelligence a sense of physical embodiment. The AI learned to associate digital sensor data with concepts like 'rough,' 'smooth,' 'hot,' and 'cold' without being explicitly programmed.

How it works: The team trained the AI using millions of sensor readings paired with human descriptions of physical sensations. The breakthrough came when the AI began generating its own descriptions of new textures and temperatures that matched human experiences with 94% accuracy, suggesting it developed genuine understanding rather than pattern matching.

Why it matters: This research could lead to AI systems that truly understand the physical world through direct experience rather than just processing text descriptions. Applications range from more intuitive robotics to AI that can better help humans with physical tasks, marking a major step toward artificial general intelligence with embodied understanding.
