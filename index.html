March 11 Deadline Looms as FTC Prepares AI Enforcement Framework

The countdown: Trump's December executive order gave the FTC until March 11, 2026 to issue a policy statement on how the FTC Act applies to AI. The Commerce Secretary faces the same deadline to identify "burdensome" state AI laws for potential federal preemption.

Colorado delayed: The Colorado AI Act, which requires deployers of high-risk AI to use "reasonable care" against algorithmic discrimination, was pushed from February 1 to June 30, 2026. The law mandates impact assessments, transparency disclosures, and documentation of AI decision-making.

The federal-state tension: The AG's new AI litigation task force is preparing to challenge state laws deemed inconsistent with federal policy. Research, Translated

DeepMind Open-Sources AlphaGenome — The AlphaFold of DNA

The breakthrough: Google DeepMind released AlphaGenome, an AI model that predicts the function of DNA sequences. Following AlphaFold's Nobel Prize-winning success with proteins, AlphaGenome represents a major leap in genomics -- and DeepMind has open-sourced the model for researchers worldwide.

Self-verifying agents: The biggest obstacle to scaling AI agents -- error accumulation in multi-step workflows -- is being solved by self-verification. Instead of human oversight at every step, agents now use internal feedback loops to check their own work. This enables complex, reliable "auto-judging" workflows.

World models are coming: LLMs predict the next word, but don't understand the physical world. Researchers believe the next leap will come from world models -- AI that learns how objects move and interact in 3D space. Signs are multiplying that 2026 will be the breakout year for this paradigm.

Adaline Labs, InfoWorld, Microsoft Research
