The problem: User studies reveal that AI-powered search engines like Perplexity and Bing Chat are paradoxically making information discovery harder by providing exhaustively detailed responses. The data: UX research firm Nielsen found that 68% of users prefer traditional Google's simple link format for quick queries over AI search's paragraph-long explanations. The fixes: Search companies are now testing 'brevity modes' and layered information architecture that reveals details progressively. Perplexity announced a 'Quick Answer' feature launching next month, while Microsoft is experimenting with confidence scores to help users gauge when to stop reading and act on information.
