AI Learns to Control Dreams by Reading Brain Signals During Sleep

The discovery: Researchers at MIT and Harvard developed an AI system that can detect when someone enters REM sleep and influence their dream content through targeted audio cues. The system monitors brain waves, identifies dream states, and plays specific sounds or words that get incorporated into the sleeper's dreams with 78% accuracy.

How it works: The AI learned to recognize neural patterns associated with different dream themes by training on data from 200 volunteers who kept detailed dream journals. During sleep, it detects when dreamers enter specific mental states and delivers precisely timed audio prompts below the conscious hearing threshold. The brain integrates these cues into ongoing dream narratives.

The applications: Beyond the obvious sci-fi implications, researchers see potential for treating PTSD nightmares, enhancing learning during sleep, and studying consciousness itself. The system could help people practice skills in dreams or overcome phobias in a controlled mental environment. However, the ethical implications of manipulating someone's subconscious experience remain largely unexplored.
