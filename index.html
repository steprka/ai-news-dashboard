The law: California Governor Gavin Newsom signed the AI Worker Displacement Protection Act, requiring companies with over 100 employees to provide 180 days advance notice before implementing AI systems that could eliminate jobs, plus mandatory retraining programs for affected workers. The requirements: Companies must conduct 'AI impact assessments' before deployment and demonstrate that they've explored alternatives to job elimination, such as role transitions or reduced working hours. The ripple effect: Business groups warn the law could drive companies to relocate operations outside California, while labor unions hail it as a model for other states.
