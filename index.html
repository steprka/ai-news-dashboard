The reaction: The move is seen as escalating the AI Cold War between China and the West, with some experts warning it could lead to completely divergent AI ecosystems. Research, Translated

AI Learns to Predict Weather with Just 12 Hours of Training

The breakthrough: Researchers at MIT developed an AI system that can match the accuracy of traditional weather models after training on just 12 hours of historical data, compared to the decades of information typically required. The system uses a novel 'physics-informed' neural network that combines basic atmospheric science with pattern recognition to make predictions.

Why this matters: Current weather prediction requires massive supercomputers running for hours to generate forecasts, limiting updates and consuming enormous energy. This AI approach could enable real-time weather updates on smartphones and make accurate forecasting accessible to developing countries that can't afford traditional meteorological infrastructure.

The real-world test: In trials covering 50 cities worldwide, the AI matched or exceeded human meteorologists' 7-day forecasts 84% of the time while using 1000x less computational power. The team is now working with weather services in Kenya and Bangladesh to deploy pilot systems that could provide life-saving storm warnings in regions with limited forecasting capabilities.
